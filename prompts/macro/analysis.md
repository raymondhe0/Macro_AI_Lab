**2. Surprise Assessment**

**3. Transmission Path**
Trace the cause-and-effect chain from the event to its final market impact. Identify the correct path for THIS event — do not force-fit a single template.

Common paths include (but are not limited to):
- `Event → Inflation expectations → Fed policy repricing → Yields → Equity valuations`
- `Event → Energy/commodity prices → Input costs → Margins → Sector rotation`
- `Event → Risk premia / safe-haven flows → Gold, JPY (Japanese Yen), CHF (Swiss Franc), Treasuries`
- `Event → Credit conditions → Lending/growth outlook → Cyclicals vs. Defensives`
- `Event → USD (US Dollar) strength/weakness → EM debt / Carry trades → Global risk appetite`
- `Event → Supply chain disruption → Goods inflation → Consumer spending`

Structure your analysis as:
- **Direct Impact:** Which assets, sectors, or instruments react first? Direction and magnitude.
- **Transmission Mechanism:** Through what channel does this propagate? (inflation, credit, FX (foreign exchange), risk appetite, etc.)
- **Downstream Effect:** Where does the chain end? What is the net effect on broad equity indices (S&P 500, Nasdaq), fixed income, and commodities?
- **Time Horizon:** Is this an intraday catalyst, a multi-day repricing, or a structural theme shift?
